Pacing the 8 × 1km runs

  • Anchor pace: your between-station pace should be 15–25 s/km slower than your fresh standalone 5k pace. As a first-timer, err conservative — you can always speed up after Run 6.
  • Run 1 is a lie. Adrenaline + fresh legs make it feel easy. Run it as your slowest planned run. Going out 20s/km too fast here costs minutes at wall balls.
  • First 200m rule: the first 200m out of every station is for resetting — shake out arms, lower heart rate, settle breathing. Build to pace, don't sprint out of the roxzone.
  • Even-split target: aim for runs 2–7 within a 10-second band of each other. Run 8 is free — empty the tank into wall balls.
  • After heavy-leg stations (sled push, sled pull, lunges): expect the first 400m to feel terrible. Shorten stride, raise cadence, it passes.
  • Walking is a tool, not a failure: 10 brisk walking steps with purpose beats a shuffle-jog spiral. Use it deliberately after sleds if needed.

Weekly recovery protocol

  • Sleep: 7.5–9h. This is 80% of your recovery. Non-negotiable in Block 2–3 when volume peaks.
  • Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg bodyweight daily. With 9 years of training you know this — don't let it slip when running volume climbs.
  • Post-session (within 60 min): 30–40g protein + 60–100g carbs after hybrid/sim days. Strength days: normal meals are fine.
  • Easy day discipline: Zone 2 means conversational. If you can't talk in full sentences, you're stealing from tomorrow's session.
  • Weekly minimums: 1 full rest day, 10 min daily mobility (hips, ankles, thoracic — your overhead weakness lives in the T-spine), 1 longer mobility session (20–30 min) on Sunday.
  • Soft tissue: calves and quads twice weekly (foam roll/massage gun) once running passes 20 km/week — this is where new HYROX runners break.
  • Monitor: resting HR each morning. 5+ bpm above baseline for 2 days = swap the next hard session for Zone 2.

Deload weeks (W5 and W10)

  • Volume: cut total volume ~40–50%. Keep some intensity (a few heavy-ish singles/doubles feel-good sets) so you don't go flat.
  • Running: all easy. No intervals. Strides only.
  • Purpose: deloads are where adaptation happens. Athletes who skip W5/W10 deloads show up to Block 3 cooked. Trust the plan.
  • Feel great by Thursday? Good. That's the point. Still don't add work.
Taper (W12–13): volume drops ~50% in W12 and ~70% in W13, intensity stays sharp and short. Expect to feel flat early in the taper and springy by race Saturday — that's normal supercompensation. Resist the panic-training urge.

Race-week checklist

    Fueling plan

    • Thu–Fri (carb load): 6–8 g carbs/kg bodyweight per day. Familiar foods only — rice, pasta, potatoes, bread, fruit. Reduce fibre and fat slightly on Friday.
    • Race morning (race is Saturday): breakfast 2.5–3h before start — ~100–150g carbs, low fibre, low fat (e.g. oats + banana + honey, white toast + jam). Sip 500ml water with electrolytes.
    • 60–90 min out: small top-up if hungry — banana or half an energy bar. Nothing new, ever.
    • 15 min before start: optional gel or sports drink sip + caffeine if you use it (3 mg/kg, only if practiced in sims).
    • During (60–90 min race): 1 gel around the rower (station 6) if the race is going past ~75 min for you. Water at stations only if genuinely thirsty — small sips.
    • Practice all of this at SIM #4 (Week 11). Race day is a dress rehearsal repeat, not an experiment.
